Backyard Brawl - Generalist Programmer

Backyard Brawl is a chaotic multi-gamemode party game, supporting 2-4 players for both local and online play. I worked on this project with seven other MSU students as a networking, gameplay and systems programming, contributing a large variety of work to the development of the game. This project will have a full steam release in late 2025.


itch.io link

Netcode

During my work on this project, I gained a plethora of valueble experience networking. This project utilized Netcode for GameObject, the Unity 6 networking solution.

I learned various strategies of optmizing online play, such as favoring the attacker in combat to create a more satisfying experience. I used a similar strategy to approach players colliding with cars. This means the collider present on the client is the one used in collision detection, and the player will only die to a car when a car appears close enough to kill them.



Modular collectible class

Backyard Brawl features a variety of collectible items, such as candy in the Candy Collectathon mode and tickets in the laserball mode. I created a modular collectible class to accomadate the different collectibles. This class contains logic for key parts of collectibles such as animations and player inventory interaction.

Power-ups and Hazards

Backyard Brawl features six unique power-ups, five of which I programmed. Each power-up required a different approach, providing interesting challenges to solve and network to maintain fairness in online play.

The gif to the left shows the "werewolf mask" power-up. This power-up gives the player a circle around representing the area of effect. Once actived, any players in this radius are stunned for a couple seconds.







I also programmed a stink bomb hazard that players may encounter. This item (gif to the right) causes players to emit a stink cloud and explodes them after 10 seconds unless they pass it onto another player by kicking them.
